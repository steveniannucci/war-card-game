Rules

It's like a typical game of War.

The player who claims all the cards or doesn't run out of them first wins.

Each time you draw a card, I will also be dealt one. Then, whichever one of us has the better card will also claim the loser's. The winner of the each match will place the cards they claimed onto the bottom of their own deck.

If we both happen to draw the same rank, we'll go to War and we'll each place three more cards on the table. The last card we put down will determine who takes all the cards in the current match. If the last cards are the same rank again, we'll flip a coin. The winner of the coin flip takes all the cards.

Oh yeah, just to make sure neither of us get bored, we'll up the ante as the game goes on. Try not to run out of cards before me.

My Additonal Rules

Jacks Are Weakest

Throughout the game, Jacks are the weakest rank (instead of the Two). You'll have to enter War with another Jack if you draw it to win matches.

Up The Ante: Three Coin Flips For Victory

This will really spice things up. Instead of just using them to decide who wins the tiebreaker in War, we can both flip coins if we were to lose a match to decide who wins in general (except during the tiebreaker since we're already flipping a coin). We'll each only get to do this three times. There's no risk to using coins but I would save them for War, you know, if you're going to lose that.

Up The Ante: Double Wars

Every time we enter War, there is a 50% chance we'll hold a Double War instead. When that happens, we'll throw down three more cards during the War. The last card in our stacks will decide the match. I'll flip a coin regardless if the ranks of those cards are the same.

Up The Ante: Double Matches

There's a 25% chance that a match will end up being a Double Match. We would draw two cards during the match instead of one. The combined rank of both cards will decide who wins. If the combined rank of each of our sides is equal, we'll go to War. Otherwise, the stronger side wins all the cards.
