Rules

It's like a typical game of War.

The player who claims all the cards or doesn't run out of them first wins.

Each time you draw a card, I will also be dealt one. Then, whichever one of us has the better card will also claim the loser's. The winner of the each match will place the cards they claimed onto the bottom of their own deck.

If we both happen to draw the same rank, we'll place three more cards into our war stacks. The last card in the stack will determine who takes all the cards in the current match. If the last cards are the same rank again, we'll flip a coin. The winner takes all the cards.

Oh yeah, just to make sure neither of us get bored, we'll up the ante more as the game goes on. Try not to run out of cards before me.

You're playing against a real Ace of Spades here.

My Additonal Rules

Jacks Are Weakest

Throughout the game, Jacks are the weakest rank. You'll have to enter War with another Jack if you draw it to win matches.

Up The Ante: Double Risk War

After a few matches, we'll start throwing down four facedown cards during War instead of two. We'll each reveal a fifth card that will decide the match. I'll flip a coin regardless if the ranks of those cards are the same. The winner will recieve all 12 cards at the end of the match.

Up The Ante: 2 vs 2 Matches

A few more matches after that, we'll up the ante even further by drawing two cards each match instead of one. If we happen to have at least one of the same card, we'll go to War. Otherwise, we'll do the math and combine the ranks of both cards on each of fields. If one of us happens to draw two of the same card, if that card's rank would be weaker than the cards of the other field, the player with the same ranking cards automatically wins the match. If we both have the same ranking cards on our respective fields, like I have Kings and you have Aces, the higher rank wins by default.

Up The Ante: Three Flips To The Top

This will really spice things up. Instead of just using them to decide who wins the tiebreaker in War, we can both flip coins to decide who wins a match in general (except during the tiebreaker since we're already flipping a coin). We'll each only get to do this three times. There's no risk to using them but I would save them for War, you know, if you're going to lose that.
