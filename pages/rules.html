Rules

It's like a typical game of War.

The player who claims all the cards or doesn't run out of them first wins.

Each time you draw a card, I will also be dealt one. Then, whichever one of us has the better card will also claim the loser's. The winner of the each match will place the cards they claimed onto the bottom of their own deck.

If we both happen to draw the same rank, we'll place three more cards into our war stacks. The last card in the stack will determine who takes all the cards in the current match. If the last cards are the same rank again, we'll flip a coin. The winner takes all the cards.

Oh yeah, just to make sure neither of us get bored, we'll up the ante as the game goes on. Try not to run out of cards before me.

You're playing against a real Ace of Spades here.

My Additonal Rules

Jacks Are Weakest

Throughout the game, Jacks are the weakest rank. You'll have to enter War with another Jack if you draw it to win matches.

Up The Ante: Three Flips For Victory

This will really spice things up. After a few matches (on Normal difficulty), instead of just using them to decide who wins the tiebreaker in War, we can both flip coins if we were to lose a match to decide who wins in general (except during the tiebreaker since we're already flipping a coin). We'll each only get to do this three times. There's no risk to using coins but I would save them for War, you know, if you're going to lose that. These coin flips will become an option starting on Hard difficulty.

Up The Ante: Double Ante Wars

A few more matches after that (on Hard difficulty), we'll start throwing down three more cards for the rest of the game during War. The sixth card in our war stacks will decide the match. I'll flip a coin regardless if the ranks of those cards are the same. The winner will recieve all fourteen cards from the match. Double Ante Wars will start on Very Hard difficulty.

Up The Ante: Double Ante Matches

We'll up the ante even further (on Extreme difficulty) by drawing two cards each match instead of one. The combined strength of both cards will decide each match. If the combined strength of each of our sides is equal, we'll go to War. Otherwise, the stronger side wins all four cards.
